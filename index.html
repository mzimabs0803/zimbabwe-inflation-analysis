Analysis: The Decadal Economic Cycle

1. Stability & Deflation (2010–2016)

Following the 2008 hyperinflation crisis, the adoption of a multi-currency system anchored the economy. During this era, inflation remained low, averaging approximately 3% (World Bank, 2026). By 2015, a strengthening US Dollar and severe liquidity shortages pushed the nation into a deflationary phase of -2.4% (FRED, 2026).

2. Political Transition & Bond Notes (2017–2018)

The year 2017 marked a pivotal turning point both politically and economically. The impeachment proceedings and subsequent resignation of Robert Mugabe created a vacuum of both leadership and market certainty. To combat a growing shortage of physical USD, the government increased the circulation of "Bond Notes." While officially pegged 1:1, the political upheaval caused market confidence to vanish, triggering a climb in prices that moved the economy out of deflation and into rising double-digit inflation (IMF, 2024).

3. Currency Re-introduction & Hyperinflation (2019–2023)

The 2019 move to abolish the multi-currency system in favor of the RTGS dollar caused an immediate currency collapse. Annual inflation rates skyrocketed, reaching a staggering 557.2% in 2020 (St. Louis Fed, 2026). Despite a brief period of "blended" rates used to mask volatility, the lack of a stable anchor led to chronic price increases for nearly half a decade.

4. The ZiG Monetary Reform (2024–Present)

In April 2024, the Reserve Bank of Zimbabwe launched the Zimbabwe Gold (ZiG). This structured currency is backed by physical reserves, including 2.5 tonnes of gold (RBZ, 2024). Projections indicate a sharp stabilization toward 12% by late 2026, though the success of this phase depends entirely on maintaining the gold-to-currency ratio and avoiding unbacked money supply growth.
